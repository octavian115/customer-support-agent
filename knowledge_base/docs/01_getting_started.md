# Getting Started with TaskFlow

## What is TaskFlow?

TaskFlow is a project management platform designed for teams of all sizes. It helps you organize work into projects, assign tasks to team members, set deadlines, and track progress — all in one place.

## Creating Your Account

Sign up at app.taskflow.io with your email address or Google/GitHub SSO. After verifying your email, you'll be prompted to create your first workspace.

## Setting Up a Workspace

A workspace is your team's home in TaskFlow. Each workspace has its own projects, members, and settings.

1. Click "Create Workspace" from the dashboard.
2. Enter a workspace name (e.g., your company or team name).
3. Choose a URL slug (e.g., taskflow.io/your-team).
4. Select your timezone — this affects due date calculations and notification timing.

You can belong to multiple workspaces. Switch between them using the workspace switcher in the top-left corner.

## Inviting Team Members

Go to Workspace Settings → Members → Invite. Enter email addresses separated by commas. You can assign one of three roles:

- **Admin**: Full control over workspace settings, billing, and member management.
- **Member**: Can create and manage projects, tasks, and comments.
- **Viewer**: Read-only access to all projects and tasks. Cannot create or edit.

Invitations expire after 7 days. You can resend from the same settings page.

## Creating Your First Project

1. Click "+ New Project" from the sidebar.
2. Choose a template (Kanban, Sprint Board, Simple List) or start blank.
3. Name your project and optionally add a description.
4. Set project visibility — "Workspace" (all members can see) or "Private" (invite-only).

Once created, you'll see your empty board. Start by creating lists (columns) like "To Do," "In Progress," and "Done," then add tasks to each list.

## Quick Tips

- Use keyboard shortcut `Q` to quick-add a task from anywhere.
- Press `Ctrl+K` (or `Cmd+K` on Mac) to open the command palette for fast navigation.
- Star important projects for quick access from the sidebar.
- TaskFlow auto-saves everything — no need to manually save.
